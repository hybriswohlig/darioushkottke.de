Step-by-Step Carbon Footprint Calculation

Step 1: Upcycling Cashew Shells (Avoided Landfill Emissions)

Yield = 60% ‚Üí Shells required = 0.0258 / 0.6 = 0.043 kg

Total diverted = 0.043 + 0.0172 = 0.0602 kg
Landfill factor = 0.1 kg CO‚ÇÇe/kg
Avoided emissions = 0.0602 √ó 0.1 = 0.006 kg CO‚ÇÇe

Step 2: Transportation of Shells

Distance = 1,000 km (sea freight)
Emissions factor = 0.016 kg CO‚ÇÇe/tonne-km
Mass transported = 0.043 kg shells per roll
Emissions added = (0.043 / 1,000) √ó 0.016 √ó 1,000 = 0.000688 kg CO‚ÇÇe
Note: Minimal impact; offset by upcycling benefits

Step 3: Production (Cradle-to-Gate)

Conventional Plastic Wrap = 2.58 kg √ó 2.03 kg CO‚ÇÇe/kg = 5.24 kg CO‚ÇÇe

Net after upcycling credit = 5.16 - 0.006 = 5.154 kg CO‚ÇÇe
Savings = 5.24 - 5.154 = 0.086 kg CO‚ÇÇe

Appendix: Detailed Calculation Formulas

A. Upcycling Credit Calculation

Given: ‚Ä¢ Plant-based component per roll (V) = 0.0258 kg ‚Ä¢ Processing yield (Y) = 60% = 0.6 ‚Ä¢ Landfill emission factor (EF_landfill) = 0.1 kg CO‚ÇÇe/kg Calculations: 1. Shells required (S) = V / Y = 0.0258 / 0.6 = 0.043 kg 2. Residue for packaging (R) = S √ó (1 - Y) = 0.043 √ó 0.4 = 0.0172 kg 3. Total shells diverted (T) = S + R = 0.043 + 0.0172 = 0.0602 kg 4. Upcycling credit (UC) = T √ó EF_landfill = 0.0602 √ó 0.1 = 0.006 kg CO‚ÇÇe Assumption: 100% landfill baseline without partnership (requires Intersnack verification)

B. Transportation Emissions

Given: ‚Ä¢ Distance (D) = 1,000 km (Tay Ninh, Vietnam ‚Üí Guangdong, China) ‚Ä¢ Sea freight emission factor (EF_sea) = 0.016 kg CO‚ÇÇe/tonne-km ‚Ä¢ Shells transported per roll (S) = 0.043 kg = 0.000043 tonnes Calculation: Transport emissions (TE) = S √ó D √ó EF_sea = 0.000043 √ó 1,000 √ó 0.016 = 0.000688 kg CO‚ÇÇe ‚âà 0.0007 kg CO‚ÇÇe Note: Minimal impact; local processing could eliminate entirely

C. Production Emissions (Cradle-to-Gate)

Given: ‚Ä¢ Roll weight (W) = 2.58 kg ‚Ä¢ Conventional plastic wrap emission factor (EF_conv) = 2.03 kg CO‚ÇÇe/kg ‚Ä¢ VIKANG Composite emission factor (EF_composite) = 2.00 kg CO‚ÇÇe/kg Calculations: 1. Conventional production (P_conv) = W √ó EF_conv = 2.58 √ó 2.03 = 5.24 kg CO‚ÇÇe 2. VIKANG production (P_composite) = W √ó EF_composite = 2.58 √ó 2.00 = 5.16 kg CO‚ÇÇe 3. Net VIKANG (after upcycling credit) = P_composite - UC = 5.16 - 0.006 = 5.154 kg CO‚ÇÇe 4. Production savings = P_conv - Net VIKANG = 5.24 - 5.154 = 0.086 kg CO‚ÇÇe

D. End-of-Life Emissions

Given: ‚Ä¢ Roll weight (W) = 2.58 kg ‚Ä¢ Theoretical CO‚ÇÇ per kg material (ThCO‚ÇÇ) = 1.41 kg CO‚ÇÇ/kg ‚Ä¢ Biodegradation rate (B) = 39.1% = 0.391 ‚Ä¢ Conventional disposal: 100% incineration ‚Ä¢ VIKANG Composite disposal: Partial biodegradation (releases B √ó ThCO‚ÇÇ) Calculations: 1. Conventional EOL (EOL_conv) = W √ó ThCO‚ÇÇ = 2.58 √ó 1.41 = 3.64 kg CO‚ÇÇ 2. VIKANG EOL (EOL_composite) = W √ó (ThCO‚ÇÇ √ó B) = 2.58 √ó (1.41 √ó 0.391) = 1.42 kg CO‚ÇÇ 3. EOL savings = EOL_conv - EOL_composite = 3.64 - 1.42 = 2.22 kg CO‚ÇÇ Assumption: Industrial composting conditions; real-world may differ

E. Total Lifecycle Comparison

Conventional Plastic Wrap Total: Total_conv = P_conv + EOL_conv = 5.24 + 3.64 = 8.88 kg CO‚ÇÇe VIKANG Composite Total: Total_composite = (P_composite - UC) + EOL_composite + TE = 5.154 + 1.42 + 0.0007 = 6.575 kg CO‚ÇÇe TOTAL SAVINGS PER ROLL: Savings = Total_conv - Total_composite = 8.88 - 6.575 = 2.305 kg CO‚ÇÇe = 26.0% reduction 2025 Annual Impact (931,000 rolls): Annual_savings = Savings √ó Volume / 1,000 = 2.305 √ó 931,000 / 1,000 = 2,146 tonnes CO‚ÇÇe

Disclaimer & Limitations

Important Notice: This report is based on LCA principles, third-party biodegradation testing (ASTM D6400), and conservative estimates. Actual emissions may vary based on regional factors, disposal methods, and supply chain variations.

Key Limitations

1. Data Quality & Assumptions

  • Shell Disposal Baseline: Assumes 100% landfill for shells without upcycling. Actual Intersnack methods (burning, composting, sale) not verified. Affects upcycling credit (¬±0.006 kg CO‚ÇÇe/roll).
  • Production Emissions: Uses European averages (PlasticsEurope, 2023). Actual Chinese facility emissions may differ. Internal data required for precision.
  • End-of-Life Scenarios: Assumes 100% incineration (conventional) or 39.1% biodegradation (VIKANG Composite) based on ASTM D6400 industrial composting. Real-world disposal in EU/Australia hotels likely differs‚Äîrequires waste audit data.

2. Geographic & Temporal Scope

  • Regional Variability: Transport distances, energy grids, waste management vary significantly across Europe and Australia. Report uses generalized averages.
  • Temporal Constraints: Based on 2023-2025 data. Energy grid decarbonization, regulations, and supply chain changes may alter future impacts.
  • Market-Specific Disposal: Hotel practices not uniform. Some may compost (enabling biodegradation), others incinerate/landfill (limiting benefit).

3. Testing Conditions vs. Real-World

  • ASTM D6400 Conditions: Industrial composting at 58¬∞C¬±2¬∞C, aerobic, 180 days. Real-world conditions (landfills, municipal composting, marine) likely yield lower biodegradation.
  • No Anaerobic Data: Most landfills are anaerobic. ASTM D5511 testing recommended for realistic end-of-life modeling.
  • Timeframe: 180-day test may not capture full degradation potential or long-term behavior.

4. System Boundaries

  • Cradle-to-Gate + EOL: Does not include upstream cashew farming impacts, downstream distribution to end-users, or product use phase.
  • Upcycling Credit Allocation: Full credit given to shells diverted. If shells have alternative value (e.g., biofuel sale), credit should be adjusted.
  • Avoided Burden Approach: Assumes landfill baseline. Other LCA methodologies (consequential LCA) may yield different results.

  • ISO 14040/14044 Audit: Third-party verification required (planned Q4 2025-Q2 2026)
  • EU Green Claims Directive: Substantiation and independent verification mandatory
  • T√úV or Equivalent: Product-level certification recommended for biodegradability claims
