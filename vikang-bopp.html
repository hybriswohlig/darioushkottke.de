What is BOPP?

BOPP stands for Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene, a type of thermoplastic polymer film produced by stretching polypropylene in both the machine direction (MD) and transverse direction (TD). This biaxial orientation enhances its mechanical properties, such as tensile strength and elongation, as well as optical characteristics like haze and gloss.

It is commonly used in flexible packaging, labels, and tapes due to its clarity, barrier properties against moisture (WVTR) and oxygen (OTR), and printability (surface tension).

In this context, the Vi-Kang BOPP incorporates an antibacterial additive derived from cashew nut shells for enhanced antimicrobial performance.

Key Performance Highlights

  • Superior Clarity: Low haze (≤1.8%) and high gloss (≥88%) for excellent product visibility
  • Excellent Strength: High tensile strength in both directions ensures durability
  • Barrier Properties: Low WVTR and OTR provide effective moisture and oxygen protection
  • Printability: High surface tension (≥38 Dyne) ensures excellent ink adhesion
  • Dimensional Stability: Low thermal shrinkage maintains form during processing
  • Antimicrobial Enhancement: Cashew nut shell additive provides antibacterial properties
