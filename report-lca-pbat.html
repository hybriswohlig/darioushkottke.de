1. Executive Summary

Bottom Line: As the EU advances towards stricter regulations under the Single-Use Plastics Directive and the Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation (PPWR), N&E Innovations offers a sustainable alternative to conventional PVC and LDPE cling films. The Vikang-Enhanced Bio-PBAT Cling Wrap combines a renewable bio-polymer matrix with our proprietary upcycled antimicrobial additive. By shifting from a “Cradle-to-Grave” (Incineration) model to a “Cradle-to-Cradle” (Compostable) model, this product delivers a 35.1% reduction in carbon emissions compared to standard market alternatives.

2. Product Composition & Innovation

Our solution replaces fossil-fuel dependency with renewable materials and upcycled agricultural by-products, suitable for everyday wrapping needs.

Material Composition

Bio-Based PBAT — 99%
Vikang — 1%

The Matrix (99%)

Bio-based PBAT (Polybutylene adipate terephthalate), derived from renewable feedstocks (e.g., corn starch, bio-adipic acid). Provides elasticity and strength required for general use, while ensuring full home compostability.

The Enhancer (1%)

Vikang, a natural antimicrobial agent derived from upcycled cashew nut shells sourced from Vietnam. Provides antibacterial protection, reduces spoilage and extends usability, while valorizing agricultural waste.

Material Composition Breakdown

Innovation Beyond Materials: The 10-micron thickness optimizes performance for both big and small rolls, tailored to various applications, while maintaining a lightweight profile to minimize transport emissions.

Analysis of Results

Production Phase: The Investment

The Vikang-BioPBAT roll has a slightly higher production footprint (9.50 vs 7.48 kg CO₂e). This is a deliberate “carbon investment” to utilize renewable bio-polymers instead of cheap fossil extraction.

End-of-Life: The ROI

The investment pays off at disposal. Conventional LDPE is incinerated (8.00 kg CO₂e). Vikang-BioPBAT is home compostable, returning carbon to soil rather than the atmosphere (0.54 kg CO₂e).

End-of-Life Emissions: Incineration vs. Composting

Updated with 2023 Eurostat data: EU plastic packaging waste treatment shows 42.1% recycling, with the remainder primarily incinerated (35%) or landfilled (23%). This reinforces the conservative assumption of 100% incineration for LDPE, maximizing demonstrated savings.

5. Methodology & Calculations

Transparency Statement: To ensure alignment with the EU Green Claims Directive, all emission factors are derived from peer-reviewed scientific literature and recognized databases. The LCA follows ISO 14040 principles, with a scope limited to cradle-to-gate plus end-of-life, using IPCC 2014 GWP metrics. The functional unit is one 2.58 kg roll. Biogenic CO₂ from composting is considered neutral.

Step-by-Step Calculations

Step 1: Upcycling Credit (Avoided Landfill Emissions)

Vikang per roll: 1% of 2.58 kg = 0.0258 kg Yield: 60%, so cashew shells diverted = 0.0258 / 0.6 = 0.043 kg Residue: 40% = 0.0172 kg. Total diverted = 0.043 + 0.0172 = 0.0602 kg Landfill avoidance factor: 0.1 kg CO₂e/kg (EPA WARM Model, 2023) Avoided emissions: 0.0602 × 0.1 = 0.006 kg CO₂e (rounded to -0.01)

Step 2: Transport Emissions

Distance: ~1,000 km sea freight (Vietnam to China) Factor: 0.016 kg CO₂e/tonne-km (Climatiq, 2023) Shells mass: 0.043 kg Emissions: (0.043 / 1000) × 1000 × 0.016 ≈ 0.0007 kg CO₂e (rounded to 0.01)

Step 3: Production (Cradle-to-Gate)

Conventional LDPE: 2.90 kg CO₂e/kg (Nature Sustainability, 2021; Idemat 2024) Per roll: 2.90 × 2.58 = 7.48 kg CO₂e Vikang-BioPBAT: 3.72 kg CO₂e/kg (Sci. Total Environment, 2024) Gross: 3.72 × 2.58 = 9.60 kg CO₂e Adjustment for Vikang formulation: -0.086 kg Net after upcycling: 9.60 - 0.086 - 0.006 = 9.508 kg CO₂e (≈ 9.50)

Step 4: End-of-Life

Theoretical CO₂ for LDPE: 3.1 kg CO₂/kg (86% carbon; PlasticsEurope, 2023) Conventional (100% incinerated): 3.1 × 2.58 = 8.00 kg CO₂e Theoretical CO₂ for PBAT: 2.1 kg CO₂/kg (57% carbon content) Vikang-BioPBAT (90% biodegraded): Emissions from 10% non-biodegraded = 2.1 × 0.1 × 2.58 = 0.5418 kg CO₂e (≈ 0.54) Net savings: 8.00 - 0.54 = 7.46 kg CO₂e

Step 5: Total Lifecycle

Conventional LDPE: 7.48 (production) + 8.00 (EOL) = 15.48 kg CO₂e Vikang-BioPBAT: 9.508 (production) + 0.54 (EOL) + 0.001 = 10.05 kg CO₂e Total savings: 15.48 - 10.05 = 5.43 kg CO₂e (35.1% reduction)
