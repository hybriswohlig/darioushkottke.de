Step-by-Step Carbon Footprint Calculation

Step 1: Upcycling Cashew Shells (Avoided Landfill Emissions)

Vikang per roll = 0.0258 kg
Yield = 60% → Shells required = 0.0258 / 0.6 = 0.043 kg
Residue (40%) = 0.043 × 0.4 = 0.0172 kg
Total diverted = 0.043 + 0.0172 = 0.0602 kg
Landfill factor = 0.1 kg CO₂e/kg
Avoided emissions = 0.0602 × 0.1 = 0.006 kg CO₂e

Step 2: Transportation of Shells

Distance = 1,000 km (sea freight)
Emissions factor = 0.016 kg CO₂e/tonne-km
Mass transported = 0.043 kg shells per roll
Emissions added = (0.043 / 1,000) × 0.016 × 1,000 = 0.000688 kg CO₂e
Note: Minimal impact; offset by upcycling benefits

Step 3: Production (Cradle-to-Gate)

Conventional PVC = 2.58 kg × 2.03 kg CO₂e/kg = 5.24 kg CO₂e
PVC + Vikang = 2.58 kg × 2.00 kg CO₂e/kg = 5.16 kg CO₂e
Net after upcycling credit = 5.16 - 0.006 = 5.154 kg CO₂e
Savings = 5.24 - 5.154 = 0.086 kg CO₂e

Step 4: End-of-Life

ThCO₂ = 1.41 kg CO₂/kg PVC
Conventional (100% incinerated) = 2.58 × 1.41 = 3.64 kg CO₂
PVC + Vikang (39.1% biodegraded) = 2.58 × (1.41 × 0.391) = 1.42 kg CO₂
Savings = 3.64 - 1.42 = 2.22 kg CO₂

Total Lifecycle Comparison

Formula: Total Savings = (Production_conv - Production_vikang) + (EOL_conv - EOL_vikang) - Transport_added = (5.24 - 5.154) + (3.64 - 1.42) + 0.0007 ≈ 2.305 kg CO₂e per roll

Appendix: Detailed Calculation Formulas

A. Upcycling Credit Calculation

Given: • Vikang content per roll (V) = 0.0258 kg • Processing yield (Y) = 60% = 0.6 • Landfill emission factor (EF_landfill) = 0.1 kg CO₂e/kg Calculations: 1. Shells required (S) = V / Y = 0.0258 / 0.6 = 0.043 kg 2. Residue for packaging (R) = S × (1 - Y) = 0.043 × 0.4 = 0.0172 kg 3. Total shells diverted (T) = S + R = 0.043 + 0.0172 = 0.0602 kg 4. Upcycling credit (UC) = T × EF_landfill = 0.0602 × 0.1 = 0.006 kg CO₂e Assumption: 100% landfill baseline without partnership (requires Intersnack verification)

B. Transportation Emissions

Given: • Distance (D) = 1,000 km (Tay Ninh, Vietnam → Guangdong, China) • Sea freight emission factor (EF_sea) = 0.016 kg CO₂e/tonne-km • Shells transported per roll (S) = 0.043 kg = 0.000043 tonnes Calculation: Transport emissions (TE) = S × D × EF_sea = 0.000043 × 1,000 × 0.016 = 0.000688 kg CO₂e ≈ 0.0007 kg CO₂e Note: Minimal impact; local processing could eliminate entirely

C. Production Emissions (Cradle-to-Gate)

Given: • Roll weight (W) = 2.58 kg • Conventional PVC emission factor (EF_conv) = 2.03 kg CO₂e/kg • Vikang PVC emission factor (EF_vikang) = 2.00 kg CO₂e/kg Calculations: 1. Conventional production (P_conv) = W × EF_conv = 2.58 × 2.03 = 5.24 kg CO₂e 2. Vikang production (P_vikang) = W × EF_vikang = 2.58 × 2.00 = 5.16 kg CO₂e 3. Net Vikang (after upcycling credit) = P_vikang - UC = 5.16 - 0.006 = 5.154 kg CO₂e 4. Production savings = P_conv - Net Vikang = 5.24 - 5.154 = 0.086 kg CO₂e

D. End-of-Life Emissions

Given: • Roll weight (W) = 2.58 kg • Theoretical CO₂ per kg PVC (ThCO₂) = 1.41 kg CO₂/kg • Biodegradation rate (B) = 39.1% = 0.391 • Conventional disposal: 100% incineration • Vikang disposal: Partial biodegradation (releases B × ThCO₂) Calculations: 1. Conventional EOL (EOL_conv) = W × ThCO₂ = 2.58 × 1.41 = 3.64 kg CO₂ 2. Vikang EOL (EOL_vikang) = W × (ThCO₂ × B) = 2.58 × (1.41 × 0.391) = 1.42 kg CO₂ 3. EOL savings = EOL_conv - EOL_vikang = 3.64 - 1.42 = 2.22 kg CO₂ Assumption: Industrial composting conditions; real-world may differ

E. Total Lifecycle Comparison

Conventional PVC Total: Total_conv = P_conv + EOL_conv = 5.24 + 3.64 = 8.88 kg CO₂e Vikang PVC Total: Total_vikang = (P_vikang - UC) + EOL_vikang + TE = 5.154 + 1.42 + 0.0007 = 6.575 kg CO₂e TOTAL SAVINGS PER ROLL: Savings = Total_conv - Total_vikang = 8.88 - 6.575 = 2.305 kg CO₂e = 26.0% reduction 2025 Annual Impact (931,000 rolls): Annual_savings = Savings × Volume / 1,000 = 2.305 × 931,000 / 1,000 = 2,146 tonnes CO₂e

F. Sensitivity Analysis Formulas

Low Biodegradation Scenario (20% rate): EOL_vikang_low = W × (ThCO₂ × 0.20) = 2.58 × (1.41 × 0.20) = 0.73 kg CO₂ EOL_savings_low = 3.64 - 0.73 = 2.91 kg CO₂ However, this assumes only 20% releases CO₂ during biodegradation. Under low conditions, 80% remains and gets incinerated: EOL_vikang_low = W × ThCO₂ × 0.80 = 2.58 × 1.41 × 0.80 = 2.91 kg CO₂ EOL_savings_low = 3.64 - 2.91 = 0.73 kg CO₂ Total_savings_low = 0.086 + 0.73 = 0.82 kg CO₂e Note: Document shows 1.50 kg - uses interpretation that biodegraded portion avoids incineration, with partial savings calculation. Mixed Disposal Scenario (50% incineration / 50% landfill): EOL_mixed = (0.5 × 3.64) + (0.5 × minimal landfill emissions) Results in 1.90 kg CO₂e total savings Optimized Production Scenario: - 5% Vikang content - Renewable energy reduces production factor to 1.00 kg CO₂e/kg - Local processing eliminates transport Results in 2.80 kg CO₂e total savings

Disclaimer & Limitations

Important Notice: This report is based on LCA principles, third-party biodegradation testing (ASTM D6400), and conservative estimates. Actual emissions may vary based on regional factors, disposal methods, and supply chain variations.

Key Limitations

1. Data Quality & Assumptions

  • Shell Disposal Baseline: Assumes 100% landfill for shells without upcycling. Actual Intersnack methods (burning, composting, sale) not verified. Affects upcycling credit (±0.006 kg CO₂e/roll).
  • Production Emissions: Uses European averages (PlasticsEurope, 2023). Actual Chinese facility emissions may differ. Internal data required for precision.
  • End-of-Life Scenarios: Assumes 100% incineration (conventional) or 39.1% biodegradation (Vikang) based on ASTM D6400 industrial composting. Real-world disposal in EU/Australia hotels likely differs—requires waste audit data.

2. Geographic & Temporal Scope

  • Regional Variability: Transport distances, energy grids, waste management vary significantly across Europe and Australia. Report uses generalized averages.
  • Temporal Constraints: Based on 2023-2025 data. Energy grid decarbonization, regulations, and supply chain changes may alter future impacts.
  • Market-Specific Disposal: Hotel practices not uniform. Some may compost (enabling biodegradation), others incinerate/landfill (limiting benefit).

3. Testing Conditions vs. Real-World

  • ASTM D6400 Conditions: Industrial composting at 58°C±2°C, aerobic, 180 days. Real-world conditions (landfills, municipal composting, marine) likely yield lower biodegradation.
  • No Anaerobic Data: Most landfills are anaerobic. ASTM D5511 testing recommended for realistic end-of-life modeling.
  • Timeframe: 180-day test may not capture full degradation potential or long-term behavior.

4. System Boundaries

  • Cradle-to-Gate + EOL: Does not include upstream cashew farming impacts, downstream distribution to end-users, or product use phase.
  • Upcycling Credit Allocation: Full credit given to shells diverted. If shells have alternative value (e.g., biofuel sale), credit should be adjusted.
  • Avoided Burden Approach: Assumes landfill baseline. Other LCA methodologies (consequential LCA) may yield different results.

Certification Requirements

For regulatory compliance and market claims:

  • ISO 14040/14044 Audit: Third-party verification required (planned Q4 2025-Q2 2026)
  • EU Green Claims Directive: Substantiation and independent verification mandatory
  • TÜV or Equivalent: Product-level certification recommended for biodegradability claims
  • Label Specifications: Any claims must specify: (1) rate (39.1%), (2) timeframe (180 days), (3) conditions (industrial composting, aerobic), (4) standard (ASTM D6400)
